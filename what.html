Reviews of 24

From the moment the B3 organ and Kristin Plater’s knock-you-on-your-butt voice kick out of the speakers at the top of the first track, you’re enraptured. By the time 24 closes, you’re ready to hit repeat. The masterful singing and hooky, infectious melodies on this record are absolutely undeniable.

— Performing Songwriter Magazine (Top 12 Picks)

24 is one of the best I have heard this year. “Wonder Woman” has meaningful lyrics that deserve to be heard. Plater is too talented to ignore.

— Joel Edelman, Agouti.com

“Dancing Stars” cuts loose with as big, brassy, and ballsy a post-blues rock tune as anyone I’ve heard lately — loaded with Hammond B-3, swooning guitar, and Plater’s huge, gorgeous voice. It wows.

— Bangsheet.com

Kristin Plater has made the kind of album that never wears you out… Musically, 24 provides enough variety to keep one wondering what is around the next corner.

— Rod Moody, Titan Magazine

Plater is a standout due to her remarkable pipes and equally remarkable songwriting skills… There are no throwaway songs to fast-forward past on 24.

— C. D. Di Guardia, Northeast Performer Magazine

Kristin’s soulful brand of songwriting is similar to Natalie Merchant, Sheryl Crow, and Sarah McLachlan — fresh and real. I get the sense that this is a feeling person singing about life and love, and not just a performer trying to sell a CD.

— Joel Simches, The Noise Magazine
